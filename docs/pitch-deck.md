## Slide 2 — The Problem

# Vietnamese women want to understand ingredients.
# No one explains it to them in Vietnamese.

- **51%** of Vietnamese Gen Z research ingredients before buying — highest in Southeast Asia
- Every ingredient tool (CosDNA, SkinCarisma, INCIDecoder) is in **English or Korean**
- Vietnamese e-commerce (Shopee, Hasaki, TikTok Shop) is a catalog — **zero explanation layer**

> The gap between what consumers want to know and what the market gives them is the opportunity.

- K-beauty = **30%** of all imported cosmetics — largest single origin
- **95%** of urban women (25–45) use skincare regularly, spending ~$30/month

## Slide 4 — The Consumer

# She already knows what niacinamide is.

## Slide 7 — How It Works

# Concern → Ingredient → Product → Buy

```
1. User selects a skin concern
   ("Mụn & lỗ chân lông" / Acne & clogged pores)

2. Kwip shows the key ingredients that address it
   (BHA, Niacinamide, Tea Tree — and why each one works)

3. Products are shown by routine step
   (Cleanser → Toner → Serum → Moisturizer → Sunscreen)

4. Each product card explains why it fits the concern

5. User clicks through to purchase (Hasaki / Shopee)
```

## Slide 8 — The Business Model

# Earn only when users actually buy.

| Phase | Model | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Now–Q3 2026 | Zero revenue | Build trust first |

| 2027+ | Premium tier | Personalized routine builder, skin tracking |

> Starting with affiliate keeps us honest.
> We only earn if our recommendations are actually good.

## Slide 10 — The Defensibility

# Four things that compound over time.

1. **Vietnamese-language ingredient content** — takes time to produce well. A late entrant starts from zero.
2. **Concern-first architecture** — not how any existing player is built. Rebuilding takes time.
3. **User personalization data** — the longer a user is on Kwip, the harder she is to replace.
4. **First-mover trust** — in a trust-based category, the first trusted tool is very hard to displace.
