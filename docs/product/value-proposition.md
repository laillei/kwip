# Kwip — Value Proposition
**Date:** March 13, 2026

---

## Segment 1: The Ingredient Researcher (Primary)

**Profile:** Vietnamese urban woman, 20–32. Already ingredient-literate — knows niacinamide, BHA, centella by name. Discovers K-beauty on TikTok but researches before buying. Frustrated every ingredient tool is in English. Wants a complete routine, not just one product.

### 1. Who
Vietnamese urban woman, 20–32. Ingredient-literate, TikTok-driven discovery, English-tool frustrated, routine-builder mindset.

### 2. Why
Build a K-beauty routine that works for her specific skin concern. Job: confidently assemble the right 4–5 products, understand why each belongs, know the application order.

### 3. What Before
- Finds products on TikTok with no structured way to verify the ingredient logic
- Opens CosDNA or INCIDecoder — English only, no concern framing, no purchase path
- Checks Reviewty for safety ratings — product-first, no routine logic
- Asks in Facebook beauty groups — inconsistent, agenda-driven, slow
- Builds routine by trial and error across 3–4 apps and 2–3 tabs

### 4. How
- Selects concern (acne, brightening, etc.) — page reconfigures instantly
- Sees key ingredients for that concern with one-line Vietnamese explanations of *why* they work
- Browses products grouped by routine step in application order, each showing ingredient reason
- Builds personal routine in Routine Builder — picks multiple products per step
- Saves to profile, shares to Facebook/Zalo as branded image card

### 5. What After
- Understands her routine, not just her products — can explain *why* BHA is in her toner
- Builds a complete, ordered routine in one session instead of days of research
- Shares her routine as social proof — drives peer trust and Kwip's organic growth
- Returns when her concern changes or she wants to upgrade a step

### 6. Alternatives
| Alternative | Why it falls short |
|---|---|
| CosDNA / INCIDecoder | English only, no concern framing, no routine logic |
| Reviewty | Product-first, safety ratings only, not routine-building |
| TikTok KOLs | Inconsistent, brand-agenda-driven, can't save or build from it |
| Shopee/Hasaki | Catalog only, zero ingredient explanation |
| Facebook groups | Slow, unstructured, no personalization |

### Value Proposition Statement
For Vietnamese women who research K-beauty ingredients before buying, Kwip is a concern-first discovery tool that explains the ingredient science behind a complete routine — in Vietnamese. Unlike Reviewty which verifies products you already know, Kwip starts with your skin problem and builds your entire routine around it.

---

## Segment 2: The Trust-Seeker (Secondary)

**Profile:** Vietnamese urban woman, 28–40. Higher spending power. Concerned about dark spots and early aging. Skeptical of salespeople, sponsored content, and brand marketing. Wants confident purchase decisions without being misled.

### 1. Who
Vietnamese urban woman, 28–40. Higher income, skeptical of agenda-driven recommendations, prioritizes trust over trend.

### 2. Why
Invest in skincare that actually works — not waste 500,000–1,000,000 VND on something trending that doesn't fit her skin. Job: find a trustworthy source that recommends based on science, not sponsorship.

### 3. What Before
- Asks friends or reads comments — anecdote-driven, not science-driven
- Follows KOLs — suspects they're paid to recommend
- Browses Shopee reviews — volume without credibility
- Walks into Watsons — pressured by staff, no ingredient context
- Buys based on packaging and brand name — frequently regrets it

### 4. How
- Selects concern (brightening, anti-aging) — sees which ingredients address it and why
- No ratings, no sponsored rankings — editorial independence is explicit
- Vietnamese ingredient explanations at her level — not intimidating, not oversimplified
- Routine structure tells her what to buy, where it fits, and why it belongs
- Share card lets her validate with friends before committing

### 5. What After
- Understands what she's buying before buying — no post-purchase regret
- Trusts the recommendation because she can see the reasoning, not just the rating
- Builds a routine she can explain to a friend or beauty counter staff
- Returns when she's ready to add a new step or upgrade an existing one

### 6. Alternatives
| Alternative | Why it falls short |
|---|---|
| KOL recommendations | Trust gap — "is she being paid?" |
| Reviewty | Safety-focused, not concern-focused |
| Brand websites | Obvious agenda — can't trust science claims |
| Beauty staff | Pushy, brand-incentivized, no ingredient depth |

### Value Proposition Statement
For Vietnamese women who want to make confident K-beauty purchases without being misled, Kwip is the first trusted skincare advisor in Vietnamese that explains the ingredient science behind a routine — not to sell a product, but to solve a skin concern. Unlike KOLs and brand sites, Kwip has no sponsorship agenda.
